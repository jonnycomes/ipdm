An integer is a number that is either a natural number, the negative of a natural number, or zero. We denote the set of all integers by \(\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) In other words,
\begin{equation*} \mathbb{Z}=\{\ldots,-4,-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3,4,\ldots\}. \end{equation*}
Hence, \(5\in\mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) \(101\in\mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) \(0\in\mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) \(-3\in\mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) but \(0.3\not\in\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\)
